What it buys you

Every backup is tracked as a job, every run keeps its history, and every artifact is checksummed in R2. You can see exactly what was mirrored, and when.

State tracked in D1. Every job, run, and artifact is recorded as a real lifecycle.

A real job lifecycle, not a black-box cron.

Every mirror runs as tracked work you can inspect. No best-effort polling, no guessing at what happened.

Atomic job flow

Schedule, dispatch, and complete backup jobs with explicit state transitions. Nothing relies on best-effort polling.

Versioned runs

Every backup run is preserved with timestamps, status, and error context for postmortems.

Queue-backed execution

The worker orchestrates work, while the container handles cloning and upload with the right isolation boundary.

Change detection

Scheduled backups skip unchanged repos using GitHub's push timestamps, so you only store what actually moved.

Reasons to keep a mirror you control.

A second home for your code that answers to your account, on your schedule, in storage you own.

Off-GitHub copy

If GitHub is down or an account is locked, your mirror still lives in your own R2 bucket.

Own your infrastructure

Your repos sit in R2 in your Cloudflare account, not in a third-party backup vendor's.

Hands-off and scheduled

Point gitcask at a repo and it re-mirrors on your interval whenever the repo changes.

Quick setup

Cloudflare Workers, D1, R2, and Queues are the only moving parts. The rest is a straightforward admin API.
